My Story

One suitcase. Four years.

I grew up in Pakistan. I never imagined I would leave my country to study overseas. Then in 2020, my father made the decision — I was going to the United States for my Bachelor's degree.

I was 18. I packed my suitcase, travelled alone for the first time without my family, and flew to a city I had never heard of — Cleveland, Ohio. I was the first person in my family to ever move to the US. I didn't know a single person in the States. I didn't have a roadmap. I just knew I was willing to work harder than anyone around me.

My first year was spent figuring out the bus routes, where to find Halal food, how to manage money, and how to find a campus job. I faced every challenge that comes with building a life from zero in a foreign country. And I kept going.

One summer I returned to Pakistan and landed an internship at the Islamabad Marriott — managing a cross-functional team across four departments and watching a framework I designed help deliver 20% revenue growth. That felt like proof of something.

I came back to Cleveland, picked up where I left off, and in 2025 earned a placement inside City Hall — the City of Cleveland's Division of Taxation. Most students my age were doing simulations. I was processing real compliance files, coordinating across real city departments, and learning what it actually means to work inside a government institution. That experience stays with me differently than anything I studied in a classroom.

I'm not from here. I don't have connections or a network.

What I have is the work — and the belief that if I put it in front of the right people, it will speak for itself.

I'm looking for a company that sponsors H1B and wants someone who will outwork everyone in the room. I want someone to put their trust in me — so I can prove it was worth it.
